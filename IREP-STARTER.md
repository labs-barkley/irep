# IREP Starter

## Change one hiring process by next Tuesday.

**Who this is for:** recruiters, talent teams and hiring managers who want fairer, more predictive selection - without waiting for new software, a bigger budget, or permission. Everything below runs on the tools you already have.

IREP (the Individual-Referential Evaluation Protocol) is an open, free standard: evaluate the person on evidence and trajectory; keep category labels away from evaluation; audit yourself afterwards. This Starter is the on-ramp: four moves you can make on your very next role.

---

## The four moves (do these now)

### 1. Strip the categories before anyone evaluates
Pick one person on your team who is *not* evaluating this role. Their job: redact every incoming application before evaluators see it. Remove name, photo, age, sex, nationality, address, and replace school and employer names with descriptors of what was actually done ("top-tier consulting firm" -> "led 4-person analytics team, 12 client engagements").

**Keep the story.** Gaps, pivots, side jobs, self-taught skills stay in - redaction removes labels, never context. A trajectory with a detour is information; a famous logo is not.

### 2. Untime the assessment
Replace the timed test or the "quick call to gauge them" with a real work sample, done on the candidate's own schedule inside a bounded window (24-72h - state it in advance). Speed is not the test unless speed is the job.

State the tool rules up front, including AI: prohibited, allowed with disclosure, or expected. An unstated rule tests candidates on their willingness to hide things.

### 3. Two evaluators, scored blind
Both score independently, without seeing each other's scores, against criteria written down *before* the first candidate was assessed. Agree in advance what happens on disagreement (discuss and document; don't quietly average). Every advance/reject decision gets one written sentence of rationale citing evidence.

### 4. Count afterwards
When the role is filled, spend 30 minutes: how many candidates entered each stage, how many passed. Then the only question that matters: **does anything about who survived each stage surprise you - compared to who applied?** Write down what you find, even (especially) if the answer is "we don't know because we can't see it." That gap is your next improvement.

---

## The three you can postpone

These belong to the full protocol and can wait for your second iteration: the **firewalled demographic channel** (lawful, voluntary category data for real equity audits - needs legal setup); **validated rubrics** (tested scoring instruments, not just written ones); and **formal Stage-4 measurement**. Without them you are not yet running full IREP - you are running the on-ramp. That's the point.

---

> **Honest benchmark:** if you do these four things consistently, your process is more rigorous than the overwhelming majority of hiring pipelines in operation today - including most of the expensively tooled ones. Structure beats software.

Once the four moves are your standard practice, you may declare: **"We follow the IREP Commitment."** (See IREP-COMMITMENT.md - the five non-negotiables.) If you claim it and don't do it, expect the commons to ask you to show your homework.
