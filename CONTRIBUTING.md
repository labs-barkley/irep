# Contributing

IREP improves in public, as it preaches. All backgrounds welcome: psychometrics, I/O psychology, ML fairness, employment law, HR practice, engineering, and — deliberately — people who have been on the wrong side of a form field.

## Where help is most needed (v0.1 → v1.0)

1. **Evidence** — additions to `evidence/bibliography.md` (peer-reviewed or large-scale field evaluations only, with a one-paragraph annotation stating which protocol principle the study grounds).
2. **Stage 2 instrument library** — validated, pre-registered assessment designs per field; validation methodology.
3. **Stage 4 audit set** — the minimal statistics an implementer must compute per pipeline stage.
4. **EU AI Act conformity cookbook** — mapping IREP principles to Annex III obligations, implementer checklist.
5. **Admissions profile** — the transposition to university/program admissions.
6. **Licensing debate** — see GOVERNANCE.md; argue your position in an issue.
7. **Translations** — French first, then others.

## How

- Open an issue before large changes; PRs for text are welcome directly.
- Every substantive claim added to the spec must carry a citation that meets the bibliography's sourcing policy.
- Discussions stay public. Protocol decisions are never made in private channels.

## Style

Declarative, precise, no hype. The spec avoids adjectives it cannot cite. Claims of effectiveness are stated with their limitations (see spec §6 for the model).

## AI-assisted contributions

Welcome, with the same rule the project itself follows: disclose the assistance, and stand personally accountable for accuracy, sourcing and originality.
