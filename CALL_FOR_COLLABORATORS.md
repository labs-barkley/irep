# Call for Collaborators

IREP is a commons. It needs three circles of people, and it needs them more than it needs code.

## 1. Pilot sites — practitioners

**Who:** Recruiters, headhunters and employers — especially those who already distrust today's tooling and have kept evaluating candidates as whole people. You are not ambassadors; you are the evidence. Your closed pipelines (Stage 1) and live requisitions (Stage 2) are where the head-to-head validation happens.

**What it involves:** A data-processing agreement, a lawful basis for anonymized historical data, and for live pilots, candidate consent flows. We bring the protocol, the analysis code, and methodological support. You get first access to results and co-authorship where contribution warrants it.

**Open an issue tagged** `pilot-site`, or write to research@irepprotocol.org for data-agreement discussions that cannot be public.

## 2. Research partners — laboratories

**Who:** University labs in labor economics, I/O psychology, psychometrics, ML fairness, employment law. IREP needs its Stage 2 instruments validated, its Stage 3 RCT designed, its analysis plans pre-registered and its claims kept honest.

**What it involves:** Methodology co-design, ethics committee processes, OSF pre-registration, publication. The protocol offers a ready-made field-experiment infrastructure (the Stage 4 measurement layer) and a citable open standard.

**Open an issue tagged** `research-partner`, or write to research@irepprotocol.org.

## 3. Voices — writers and advocates

**Who:** People who write and speak about hiring, bias, credentialism, open source and digital commons. The protocol's premise is that the research has existed for thirty years and the sector never operationalized it; closing that gap is a narrative problem as much as a technical one.

**What it involves:** Read the spec. Criticize it publicly — issues welcome, sharp ones especially. Carry the argument where we can't.

## What we will never ask of anyone

Money for access. The protocol, the tooling, the results: free, for everyone, permanently — including for commercial HR vendors building it into their products. What we refuse is the financialization of the doorway itself: access to employment, like access to education and literacy, is a gateway a society cannot equitably price.

## One rule for everyone

Claims require citations; results get published including when they're null or negative; and no human data enters this project without consent and a lawful basis. If that pace feels slow, it is — it's the speed of being right.
